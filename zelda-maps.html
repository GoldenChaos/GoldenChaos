Visit Zelda Maps

Zelda Maps began as an idea in 2013 when webmaster Danilo Passos approached me at E3 2013 with a stunning tech demo. He had created a web-based map with support for a variety of Zelda-specific features such as going through doors, browsing multiple dungeon floors, and even a 360-degree "street view" module that could be preloaded with 3D models running on WebGL.

"The new Zelda is huge, but this map breaks it down" Read more on Polygon

Initially, we thought the maps could be put to use for Zelda Universe's guides section, but the project didn't gain much momentum internally until Nintendo's announcement of The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild at E3 2015, two years later. It eventually became pretty clear that the map for this new game was going to be enormous, so we decided to start taking what little information we had available and translating it into vector maps on Danilo's platform.

Then, just days before the game's launch in early 2017, our team became the first to successfully datamine a leaked copy of Breath of the Wild. With this data, we were able to completely ditch our handmade map and use the real map from the game. We also extracted the locations of every single item, enemy, and place of interest. We quickly poured all of this information into our map's database and launched shortly it shortly after on Reddit.

Since then, Zelda Maps has been updated several times with new features, including the ability to track which items you've already collected. Check out the most recent designs for Zelda Maps below.
